Discovery

The process should begin by gaining an understanding of the product. order to create a logical solution, it is important to understand the requirements, the commercial landscape, and the current constraints that are placed on a project. information is usually compiled through stakeholder workshops, interviews, and competitive analysis.

User Insights

Business objectives should to be balanced with user needs to create the possible user experience. order to gain user insights and data, I collect information using a of user research methods including, quantitative surveys, user , card sorting exercises, and contextual inquiries.

Ideation and Design

Once the features have been prioritized, the next step is developing creative solutions. phase begins by sketching to explore design concepts and layout. hypotheses are investigated more throughly through paper , user testing, and low fidelity wireframes. several iterations, the solidified designs are made into higher wireframes and mockups that are continually tested and adjusted on user feedback.

Specifications

The previous phases have illustrated the best possible design solution the product. The final phase is collecting and concisely explaining details and requirements of the designs to stakeholders and developers. most effective ways of communicating these details are through documents, high fidelity prototypes, screen flows, site/app maps, and user flows.
